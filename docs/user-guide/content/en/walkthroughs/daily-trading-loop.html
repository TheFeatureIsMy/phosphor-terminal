Daily Trading Loop

Walkthrough · 2 / 5 · 7 min

Every day before the market opens, spend 7 minutes going through a fixed routine — quick scan the big picture, check market structure, review signals, assess risk, and decide what to do (or not do) today.

Before You Start — Checklist

  • ☐ Backend is running, Dashboard loads without errors
  • ☐ At least one strategy is active (dry-run or live), or signals are flowing in
  • ☐ Freqtrade heartbeat is normal, exchange connection is online

Flow

Steps

1 · Quick Scan (Dashboard / AI Control Tower)

Open the Dashboard. The Trading Workflow Rail at the top shows the real-time status of each step in the Daily Trading Loop — green means normal, yellow means a warning, red means a blocker. Below the rail, the P0 area shows the AI market judgment (direction + confidence), current position P&L, and the number of pending confirmations. Spend 30 seconds building a first impression of today's market.

  • What to look at: AI market judgment direction and confidence, whether position P&L is positive or negative, number of pending confirmations
  • How to decide: Clear AI direction (confidence ≥ 70%) with mostly profitable positions means you can participate actively. Low confidence or large losses signal caution.
  • Next: Regardless of what you see, proceed to the next step — Market Structure.

2 · Check Market Structure (Market Structure)

Go to the Market Structure page. Select your main trading pair's higher timeframe (start with 4H or 1H). The top area shows the current market regime label (Uptrend / Downtrend / Ranging / High Volatility / Panic) and a structure score (0-100). Below that is the event timeline — recent BoS (Break of Structure), CHoCH (Change of Character), and Sweep (Liquidity Sweep) events.

  • What to look at: The structure score and the direction of the most recent structural event
  • How to decide: Score ≥ 70 means healthy structure. If the latest event is a BoS aligned with your intended direction, it is a good entry environment. A CHoCH or Sweep suggests the market may be turning — proceed with caution.
  • Next: Score ≥ 60 with a clear trend → continue to the Structure Matrix. If score is below 40 or the market is in "Panic" mode, consider skipping trading today.

3 · Multi-Timeframe Structure Review (Structure Matrix / HTF Tribunal)

Open the Structure Matrix page. You will see a 4x4 grid — rows are timeframes (5m / 15m / 1H / 4H), columns are structure zone types (OB / FVG / LP). Each cell shows a strength percentage for that period and zone. The verdict panel on the right shows the MTF Guard state machine's final opinion: Allow, Observe, Require Confirmation, Reduce Position, or Block Entry. The countdown ring above the grid shows time remaining on the slowest candle.

  • What to look at: The verdict panel's final ruling and whether all timeframes are aligned
  • How to decide: "Allow" (green) with consistent direction across timeframes (4H and 1H both bullish/bearish) means you can operate actively. "Require Confirmation" means wait for the Signal Center to corroborate. "Block Entry" (red) means no entry — regardless of how attractive the signal looks.
  • Next: Verdict is "Allow" or "Observe" → go to the Signal Center. Verdict is "Block Entry" → the loop ends here. Wait.

4 · Review Fused Signals (Signal Center)

Enter the Signal Center. The default view shows fused signals from all sources, sorted by composite score descending. The score is a weighted average of each source strategy's performance over the last 30 days. Click a signal card to expand the detail panel showing each source's contribution weight and analysis. You can also filter by source or direction.

  • What to look at: The highest-scored signals, their direction, and risk level
  • How to decide: Score ≥ 4.0 with direction matching both the Market Structure and MTF verdict is a high-quality entry signal. Scores 2.5-4.0 are worth noting but need extra confirmation. Ignore anything below 2.5.
  • Next: If there is a high-quality signal aligned with your structure analysis → go to the Risk Center to check exposure. If no signals or they conflict with the structure read → the loop ends here for today.

5 · Assess Risk Water Level (Risk Center)

Open the Risk Center. The large arc gauge at the top shows the overall risk state: SAFE (green) / WARN (yellow) / RISK (orange) / LOCK (red) / HALT (red). Below are four risk guard cards — daily loss limit, weekly loss limit, total exposure, and consecutive losses — each showing the current consumption ratio. The emergency panel in the bottom right has "Block New Positions" and "Emergency Stop" buttons.

  • What to look at: The gauge color and state label, plus each guard's consumption percentage
  • How to decide: SAFE with all guards under 60% = normal trading. WARN with a guard near 80% = reduce new entries. RISK or LOCK = stop new entries immediately, investigate the cause. HALT = circuit breaker triggered.
  • Next: Risk state is SAFE or WARN → proceed to the Execution Center. RISK or above → stop trading and resolve the risk issue first.

6 · Act or Wait (Execution Center)

Final stop — the Execution Center. Check all running sessions: heartbeats normal, latency within threshold, position and open order counts look reasonable. If the previous steps all passed, this is usually just a confirmation that everything is fine. If you trade manually, you can open or adjust positions here. For automated strategies, confirm they are executing as expected.

  • What to look at: All session status dots (green/yellow/red), Freqtrade heartbeat, average latency
  • How to decide: All sessions green + healthy heartbeat + latency < 200ms = everything is normal. Yellow or red sessions need investigation.
  • Next: The daily loop is complete. Set up Dashboard alerts if needed, then go do something else. Come back before market close for a quick check on the Execution Center.

When You're Done — Self-Check

  • ☐ Today's market structure read (uptrend/downtrend/ranging) is noted
  • ☐ Risk Center gauge is still green (SAFE) or at most yellow (WARN)
  • ☐ Execution Center has no red abnormal sessions
  • ☐ If new positions were opened, they appear on the Orders/Positions page

If Something Goes Wrong

  • Dashboard loads slowly or shows system errors — Check the Live Readiness page first to confirm component health. Common causes: Freqtrade not started or Redis disconnected.
  • Market structure score drops from 80 to 30 suddenly — A CHoCH (structure reversal) or high-volatility event likely occurred. Switch to a lower timeframe (5m/15m) to confirm details. Consider a conservative approach today.
  • MTF verdict is "Block Entry" with no obvious reason — Open the Shadow Window panel (below the Structure Matrix) to check for hidden opposing structures on higher timeframes you might have missed.
  • Signal Center shows no high-score signals — The market may be ranging, and your strategies are not generating signals. This is normal — not every trading day has opportunities.
  • Risk gauge turns red (RISK or above) — Stop new entries immediately. Check which guard was triggered. Go to the Stop Protection page to see individual position stop-loss states. If the circuit breaker has fired, wait for auto-unlock or manually assess and reset.
