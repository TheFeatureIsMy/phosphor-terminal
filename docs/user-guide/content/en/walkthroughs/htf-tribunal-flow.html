HTF Tribunal Flow

Walkthrough · C / 5 · 8 min

After a low-timeframe structure break, walk through the full Structure Matrix + Shadow Window + HTF candle close verdict pipeline, then push confirmed parameters to Execution Center and place the order.

Before You Start — Checklist

  • ☐ A strategy is already running and has generated at least one signal
  • ☐ You understand SMC/ICT basics (BOS, CHoCH, Order Block, FVG)
  • ☐ You have completed Walkthrough · First Strategy and Walkthrough · Daily Trading Loop

Flow

Steps

1 · Signal arrives, switch to Structure Matrix (Strategy Workspace → Structure Matrix)

Your strategy catches a long signal on the 5m chart — price broke above the previous HH, a clean BOS. But you don't enter yet: low-timeframe breaks can be traps. You open the Structure Matrix to let the MTF Guard run its multi-timeframe review.

  • What to look at: The Fast Track Health bar at the top. It must be green — stale data means the verdict can't be trusted.
  • How to decide: Green means data latency < 100ms and Redis is connected. Orange or red means data may be stale — check your data sources first.
  • Next: Fast Track healthy → read the evidence matrix (step 2). Unhealthy → check Data Source Management.

2 · Read the evidence matrix (Structure Matrix)

The 4x3 matrix uses color to show each timeframe-structure zone's strength at a glance. Your BOS happened on 5m — check whether the 4H and 1H OB / FVG columns support the same direction. Green across higher timeframes means good structural alignment.

  • What to look at: Strength percentages in the 4H and 1H OB / FVG cells. Green ≥ 70%, yellow 40-70%, red < 40%.
  • How to decide: If 4H OB is green (85% strength) and 1H OB is also green (78%), the HTF structure is on your side. If 4H is red, your entry direction opposes the larger trend — high risk.
  • Next: Higher timeframes support → check the verdict panel (step 3). Higher timeframes disagree → skip this signal, return to Strategy Workspace to wait.

3 · Shadow window activates, wait for verdict (Structure Matrix)

Price broke structure on the 5m chart, but this happened inside a 4H OB zone — the fast timeframe has intruded on the slow timeframe's territory. An orange shadow window indicator bar lights up at the top of the Structure Matrix. The countdown ring starts ticking.

  • What to look at: The remaining time on the countdown ring, plus the violation count and retracement progress in the shadow window panel.
  • How to decide: The ring shows 42 minutes until the 1H candle closes. Until then, the verdict panel stays at OBSERVE — no entry allowed. If price retraces back outside the zone before the close, the shadow window may close early.
  • Next: Wait for the countdown to reach zero (step 4). Or watch the retracement progress — if price quickly moves back to safe territory, you can refresh the matrix early.

4 · HTF candle closes, verdict issued (Structure Matrix)

The 1H candle finally closes. The MTF Guard state machine evaluates where price settled and issues its final verdict. Refresh the matrix and check the verdict panel — it has changed from OBSERVE to ALLOW or BLOCK ENTRY.

  • What to look at: The verdict verb — ALLOW (green), OBSERVE (yellow), REQUIRE CONFIRMATION (yellow), REDUCE SIZE (orange), BLOCK ENTRY (red).
  • How to decide: ALLOW means HTF structure confirmed the LTF break — enter. BLOCK ENTRY means HTF structure rejected it — don't open a position. REQUIRE CONFIRMATION means you need an extra trigger (e.g. wait for the next candle's open).
  • Next: ALLOW → step 5. BLOCK ENTRY → drop the signal. REQUIRE CONFIRMATION → wait for the extra condition and check again.

5 · Push verdict parameters to Execution Center (Structure Matrix → Execution Center)

Below the verdict panel there is a "Send to Execution Center" button. Clicking it fills the order form with parameters carried by the verdict — pair, direction, entry range, stop-loss reference, and suggested position size.

  • What to look at: Whether the order form was populated correctly. Pay special attention to the entry range and stop-loss reference — they should match the Structure Matrix zones.
  • How to decide: If the verdict was REDUCE SIZE, the position size in the form should be halved automatically. Check that the size matches the verdict's recommendation.
  • Next: Parameters correct → check account status (step 6). Parameters wrong → adjust manually or re-fetch the verdict from the Structure Matrix.

6 · Check account status, submit order (Execution Center)

The Execution Center shows your account balance, open positions, and available margin on the right. Before submitting, confirm your capital can support this trade and that it doesn't conflict with existing positions.

  • What to look at: Available margin vs. order margin requirement; any existing positions in the same direction.
  • How to decide: Available margin should be at least 150% of the order's margin requirement — leave a safety buffer.
  • Next: Confirmed → submit the order. Margin too low → reduce size or skip.

7 · Track the order in Orders & Positions (Orders & Positions)

After submission, switch to Orders & Positions to monitor execution. Limit orders show as "pending," then "filled" once executed. During the life of the position, make it a habit to periodically check back on the Structure Matrix to confirm the structure is still valid.

  • What to look at: Order status (pending / partially filled / filled / canceled), and the position's unrealized P&L plus structural validity.
  • How to decide: If the MTF Guard state changes from ALLOW to OBSERVE or BLOCK ENTRY while you're in the trade, the structure has shifted — consider an early exit.
  • Next: Position running → add it to your Daily Trading Loop checklist. Position closed (TP/SL) → log it in Growth Review.

When You're Done — Self-Check

  • ☐ Your order has been submitted and shows the correct direction, size, and stop-loss on Orders & Positions
  • ☐ You know the current structural verdict (ALLOW / OBSERVE / BLOCK) for your open position
  • ☐ You understand why the shadow window matters — if you skipped steps 3-4 and entered early, you might get stopped out an hour later by an unexpected reversal

If Something Goes Wrong

  • Verdict panel stays on OBSERVE — The countdown ring may still be running; wait for it to hit zero. If it's at zero and still OBSERVE, the shadow window may have re-activated (price re-entered the violation zone).
  • Parameters copied to Execution Center don't match — Check whether the pair selector on the Structure Matrix matches the strategy signal. A mismatched pair produces wrong parameters.
  • Fast Track Health is red — A data source may be disconnected or the Redis cache may have expired. Check data connections in Data Source Management.
