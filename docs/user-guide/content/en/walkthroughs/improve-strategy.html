Improve a Strategy

Walkthrough · E / 5 · 8 min

After a week of running, use Growth Review and Failure Clustering to find your strategy's weakness, then iterate through parameter optimization and a shadow strategy dry-run.

Before You Start — Checklist

  • ☐ The strategy has been running for at least one week (dry-run or live)
  • ☐ You have at least 20 trades recorded — enough data for meaningful statistics
  • ☐ You have completed Walkthrough · First Strategy and Walkthrough · Risk Incident

Flow

Steps

1 · Open Growth Review, check the weekly equity curve (Growth Review)

Your strategy has been running for a week. You open Growth Review to see the big picture. The weekly equity curve shows daily P&L from Monday to Sunday. It might be net positive, but you notice a few noticeable drawdowns mid-week.

  • What to look at: The shape of the equity curve — smooth climb (great), jagged (normal but improvable), persistent decline (concerning). Note the timing and depth of drawdowns.
  • How to decide: If the curve trends upward with manageable drawdowns, the core logic is sound — you can optimize. If it's trending down, the strategy may need a fundamental rethink.
  • Next: Equity curve looks healthy → move to Failure Clustering to pinpoint loss patterns (step 2). Unhealthy → consider pausing the strategy and revisiting First Strategy.

2 · Failure Clustering reveals the loss pattern (Failure Clustering)

Failure Clustering automatically groups all losing trades from the week. The largest cluster is labeled "Low Liquidity Session · 02:00-05:00 · 55% loss share." Your strategy performs especially poorly during the Asian low-liquidity hours.

  • What to look at: The cluster with the highest loss share and its characteristic dimensions (time of day, market conditions, signal type, holding duration).
  • How to decide: If one cluster accounts for 50%+ of all losses, that's your optimization target. For example, if losses cluster in low-liquidity hours, adding a session filter to your strategy could help.
  • Next: Clear pattern found → go to Strategy Optimization for parameter suggestions (step 3). Clusters scattered with no clear pattern → you may need more data or longer observation.

3 · Strategy Optimization suggests parameter tweaks (Strategy Optimization)

The Strategy Optimization page reads your trade history and failure cluster results, then auto-generates parameter adjustment suggestions. For example: "Suggestion 1: Add session filter 06:00-22:00, estimated 55% loss reduction. Suggestion 2: Reduce FVG signal weight to 0.6 (currently 0.8), estimated 30% loss reduction."

  • What to look at: Each suggestion's expected impact (loss reduction %) and confidence level. Prioritize suggestions with high confidence (80%+).
  • How to decide: Don't adopt every suggestion. Pick 1-2 that align with your own analysis and have the strongest expected impact. Never change too many parameters at once — you won't know what actually worked.
  • Next: Adjustments selected → duplicate the strategy and apply changes on the copy (step 4).

4 · Backtest the adjustments (Backtest Simulation)

In the Strategy Workspace, create a copy of your current strategy, apply the selected parameter changes, and run a backtest. The backtest uses historical data to simulate how the modified strategy would have performed over the past several weeks. Compare the results against the original strategy's historical performance.

  • What to look at: Key backtest metrics — total return, max drawdown, win rate, profit factor, Sharpe ratio.
  • How to decide: The new parameters should be better or equal on both total return and max drawdown. Higher return at the cost of significantly larger drawdown is not necessarily an improvement.
  • Next: Backtest looks better → deploy as a shadow strategy (step 5). Backtest looks worse → go back to Strategy Optimization and try other suggestions.

5 · Deploy as a shadow strategy, run in parallel (Strategy Workspace)

A shadow strategy is "observe only, no orders" — it generates signals and tracks simulated P&L just like a live strategy, but never executes real trades. Create a shadow strategy with the new parameters and run it alongside the original.

  • What to look at: The shadow strategy's status shows "Shadow · Dry-Run." Confirm it uses the modified parameters you chose.
  • How to decide: The shadow and original strategies receive the same market data, so you can compare performance fairly. The shadow has zero impact on your real account.
  • Next: Shadow deployed → wait at least one week for dry-run data (step 6).

6 · Compare and decide after one week (Growth Review / Strategy Workspace)

A week later, return to Growth Review and compare the original and shadow strategies side by side. Growth Review supports overlaying two equity curves and comparing key metrics.

  • What to look at: The two equity curves overlaid — is the shadow strategy's curve consistently above the original? Is its max drawdown smaller?
  • How to decide: Shadow consistently outperforms (higher return, same or lower drawdown) → promote it to the main strategy. Performance is similar → give the shadow more time (another week). Shadow performs worse → terminate it and try a different optimization direction.
  • Next: Shadow wins → promote it in the Strategy Workspace. Archive or terminate the original. Need more time → keep both running in parallel.

When You're Done — Self-Check

  • ☐ You made at least one parameter adjustment and validated it with a backtest
  • ☐ A shadow strategy is deployed, and you know where to compare the two strategies' performance
  • ☐ You understand the "change one variable at a time" principle — changing too many parameters at once makes attribution impossible

If Something Goes Wrong

  • Backtest results differ wildly from dry-run performance — Likely overfitting. Historical backtest data doesn't guarantee future results. If the gap is large, simplify your parameter changes — fewer parameters, simpler rules.
  • Strategy Optimization generated no suggestions — Not enough trade data. You need at least 20 trades for statistically meaningful suggestions. Keep the strategy running to accumulate data.
  • Can't create a shadow strategy — Check if you've hit the maximum strategy limit. View your strategy quota in Settings.
