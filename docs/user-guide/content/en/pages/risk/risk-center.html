Risk Center

Page · Risk · 5 min

The Risk Center is AlphaLoop's risk command hub — one arc gauge tells you the system's overall risk posture, and a grid of guard cards shows which limits are approaching their threshold.

TL;DR

The Risk Center uses a large 270-degree arc gauge to display your account's overall risk state (SAFE / WARN / RISK / LOCK / HALT). Below it, four risk guard cards track the consumption ratio of daily loss, weekly loss, total exposure, and consecutive loss limits. The emergency action panel at the bottom provides "Block Entries" and "Emergency Stop" buttons. The page background subtly pulses in the current risk color.

Who uses it & when

Check the Risk Center first thing every morning, or whenever an alert fires. It tells you whether the system is safe to continue trading. If you only glance at one risk page before the session, this is it. For per-position stop management, go to the Stop Protection page instead.

Page Anatomy

  1. 1. Arc Gauge — A 270-degree arc showing the overall risk state: SAFE (green), WARN (yellow), RISK (orange), LOCK (red), HALT (deep red). A pulsing outer ring appears when the state is not normal.
  2. 2. Quick Stats — System state label, guard alert count (alerts / total), emergency lock status, and number of active reason codes.
  3. 3. Risk Guards — Each card shows one limit's current consumption: a mini progress bar, numerical value vs. limit, usage percentage, and a status dot (green / yellow / orange / red).
  4. 4. Emergency Actions — "Block Entries" stops new orders without closing existing positions. "Unblock" restores normal operation. "Emergency Stop" halts all trading and closes positions at market (requires confirmation).

Reading the Key Metrics

  • Risk State (SAFE / WARN / RISK / LOCK / HALT) — SAFE means all guards are healthy. WARN means at least one guard is approaching its threshold. RISK means multiple guards are past their thresholds. LOCK means the system has automatically locked. HALT means emergency stop has been executed.
  • Guard Consumption Percentage — For example, "Daily Loss 120 / 500 (24%)". Lower is better. Over 80% enters warning territory; over 100% is breached and triggers the corresponding circuit breaker.
  • Emergency Lock (ACTIVE / OFF) — ACTIVE means the system has been locked and no new trades or modifications are allowed. Must be manually unlocked from the emergency panel.
  • Reason Codes — When the system is in a non-normal state, specific reason codes (e.g., daily_loss_limit_reached, exchange_stop_discrepancy) appear here for diagnosis.

Common Actions, Step by Step

  1. Check overall risk — Open the Risk Center page. The arc gauge and summary stats load immediately. Green means everything is nominal.
  2. Inspect a guard — Scan the guard cards, watching for consumption percentages above 80%. Click a card to navigate to the corresponding limit configuration.
  3. Emergency stop all trading — Click the red "Emergency Stop" button → confirm in the dialog → the system halts all strategies and closes positions → the gauge changes to HALT.
  4. Unblock after incident — Once the issue is resolved, click "Unblock" to restore normal system operation.

Behind the Scenes

The Risk Center fetches data from the BFF endpoint /api/risk/overview. The backend first uses the RiskAggregator service to read real-time guard states from Redis. If Redis is unavailable or empty, it falls back to querying the risk events table in PostgreSQL. If the database is also empty, it returns mock sample data. Data is loaded once when the page opens — there is no auto-polling. The emergency action buttons call POST endpoints /api/risk/emergency-stop, /api/risk/block-new-entries, and /api/risk/unblock respectively.
