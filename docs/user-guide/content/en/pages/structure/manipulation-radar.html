Manipulation Radar

Page · Structure · 5 min

Scan any trading pair for market manipulation signals — stop hunting, holder concentration, pump & dump, and liquidity traps — and get a composite risk score with a clear trading recommendation.

TL;DR

The Manipulation Radar is an interactive market manipulation detection tool. Enter a symbol and hit Scan to get a composite manipulation score (0-100) plus four sub-scores: Stop Hunt, Holder Concentration, Pump & Dump, and Liquidity Trap. Each result can be expanded to reveal detailed evidence across five categories (candlestick anomaly, volume anomaly, funding/OI anomaly, on-chain/wallet activity, and news/KOL coordination), along with a trading recommendation (Allow, Reduce, Paper Only, or Reject).

Who uses it & when

Every trader — especially those trading altcoins and low-liquidity pairs. Scan an unfamiliar symbol before deciding to trade it. Also useful when you spot unusual price action: was that sudden spike a normal move or engineered manipulation?

Page Anatomy

  1. ① Page title — Displays "Manipulation Radar" and the current overall status (normal or anomalous signals detected).
  2. ② Search bar — Type a symbol (e.g. SOL/USDT) and press Enter or click Scan to initiate detection.
  3. ③ Stat cards — Three cards show total scanned, high risk count, and blocked count (critical risk auto-blocks trading).
  4. ④ Score row — Each scanned symbol appears as a row showing: symbol name, risk level badge, suggestion badge, composite manipulation score bar (0-100), and four sub-scores (Stop Hunt, Holder Concentration, Pump & Dump, Liquidity Trap).

Reading the Key Metrics

  • Composite Manipulation Score — 0-100. Above 70 indicates highly suspicious manipulation, 40-70 means caution, below 40 is normal. The score is a weighted combination of four sub-scores.
  • Stop Hunt — Detects whether price repeatedly spikes to critical liquidity levels to harvest stop-loss orders. A high score means the price action has a clear "hunting" pattern.
  • Holder Concentration — Analyzes on-chain holder distribution. If the top few addresses hold a very high percentage of the circulating supply, whales have the power to manipulate price.
  • Pump & Dump — Detects abnormal volume spikes accompanied by rapid price surges and dumps within short time windows. Classic PnD pattern.
  • Liquidity Trap — Detects spoofing behavior where large orders are placed to create false depth and then canceled. A high score suggests the market maker may be setting deceptive orders.
  • Suggestion Badge — Allowed = trade freely; Reduce = lower position size; Paper Only = use paper trading only; Reject = do not trade this symbol at all.

Common Actions, Step by Step

  1. Type a symbol in the search bar (e.g. AVAX/USDT) and press Enter or click Scan.
  2. Wait a few seconds — the backend analyzes multiple data sources (candles, volume, holder distribution, on-chain transfers, social sentiment).
  3. Check the composite manipulation score and risk level badge. If it says "High" or "Critical", review the sub-scores to understand where the risk comes from.
  4. Click "Expand Evidence" on a score row to see detailed evidence across five categories, each with a 0-3 severity bar.
  5. Follow the suggestion badge: if it says "Reject", do not trade this symbol live. If "Paper Only", observe in paper trading first.

Behind the Scenes

Data comes from the /api/manipulation/scores (list) and /api/manipulation/scan (scan) endpoints. The backend collects data from multiple dimensions: exchange OHLCV data for PnD and stop-hunt pattern detection; on-chain data (via blockchain APIs) for holder concentration analysis; order book snapshots for liquidity trap detection; and social media / NLP for coordinated KOL shilling detection. Results are sorted by risk level (Critical > High > Medium > Low). The mock client provides sample data for demonstration and testing.
