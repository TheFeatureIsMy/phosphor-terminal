Growth Review

Page · Growth · 5 min

Review strategy performance each week: which strategies are winning, which are losing, and what to optimize next.

TL;DR

The Growth Review page is the central dashboard for strategy performance. Run daily or weekly reviews, decompose P&L by individual strategy, evaluate signal source accuracy, and review AI-discovered optimization candidates. The page has four tabs: Analysis Reports, Candidate Strategies, SHAP Feature Importance, and Signal Validity. Select a time period (day/week/month) and the system aggregates trades from that window, computes key metrics, and generates improvement suggestions.

Who uses it & when

Every strategy author running live or paper trades. Open the Growth Review at least once a week to assess performance. Start here when you notice a losing streak or declining returns. For a quick daily check, select the "day" period to see the latest snapshot.

Page Anatomy

  1. ① Analysis Reports List — Shows generated daily/weekly/performance report cards. Each card shows the report type, date, summary preview (strategies reviewed, candidates found, top performer), and run status. Click "Run Daily Review" to generate a new report.
  2. ② Key Metric Summary — Core performance indicators per report: total return, win rate, Sharpe ratio, max drawdown. Green is good, yellow is cautionary, red signals risk. The P&L contribution of each strategy is visible at a glance.
  3. ③ Findings & Suggestions — AI-identified issues and improvement suggestions at the bottom of each report. Each finding has a severity level; suggestions can be turned into candidate strategies for further optimization.

Reading the Key Metrics

  • Total Return — Total profit percentage over the selected period. Positive is green, negative is red. Decompose by individual strategy to find the laggards dragging overall performance.
  • Sharpe Ratio — Risk-adjusted return. ≥1.5 is excellent (green), 1.0-1.5 is acceptable (yellow), <1.0 is low. A declining Sharpe trend means the strategy's risk/reward profile is deteriorating.
  • Win Rate — Percentage of profitable trades. ≥50% is green. Always read alongside profit factor — a low win rate with high profit factor can still be profitable.
  • Max Drawdown — Peak-to-trough decline in the period. Always shown in red. A sudden drawdown expansion warrants attention.
  • Signal Accuracy — In the Signal Validity tab, accuracy is broken down by source (AI Research, technical indicators, KOL, etc.). >65% green, 50-65% yellow, <50% red.

Common Actions, Step by Step

  1. Run a daily/weekly review — In the Analysis Reports tab, click "Run Daily Review". The system fetches all closed trades in the period, computes metrics, and generates a report. A new report appears at the top of the list within seconds.
  2. Select a time period — Click the period selector (day/week/month) to switch the view. Metrics are recalculated for each window, helping you observe strategy behavior at different time scales.
  3. Review candidate strategies — Switch to the "Candidates" tab. AI-generated improvement ideas are presented as strategy DSL snippets. Each candidate shows a confidence score and DSL preview. Click "Backtest" to validate or "Confirm" to promote it to a formal strategy version.
  4. Analyze SHAP feature importance — In the SHAP tab, see which market factors most influence your strategy's decisions. Longer bars mean greater impact on the final signal. Understand which conditions (volatility, volume, support/resistance distance, etc.) are driving your trades.

Behind the Scenes

When you click "Run Daily Review," the frontend calls GrowthService, which reads all closed trades from the ExecutionLedger within the specified window. The trade_analyzer computes metrics (return, win rate, Sharpe, drawdown, etc.), and report_builder generates findings and suggestions. Candidate strategies are produced by candidate_generator, which creates DSL patches based on identified weaknesses. SHAP analysis uses pre-computed feature importance models on the backend, cached in Redis. All reports and candidates are persisted to the database for later review and confirmation.
