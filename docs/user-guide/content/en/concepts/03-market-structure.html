Market Structure: BOS & CHoCH

Concept · 03 / 10 · 7 min

Market structure is the sequence of highs and lows. BOS continues the trend; CHoCH flips it.
"A Break of Structure (BOS) occurs when price breaks beyond a key swing point, confirming that the current trend is still intact. It is the foundational signal in SMC for trend continuation."— FluxCharts[1]

Why It Matters

Every high and low on a chart is a vote — buyers pushed price up, sellers pushed it down. Market structure connects these votes into a line that tells you who is in control.

BOS (Break of Structure) means the trend is healthy. Price makes a new high (in an uptrend) or a new low (in a downtrend), confirming momentum is still in that direction. Most trend-following strategies revolve around catching BOS.

CHoCH (Change of Character) warns the trend may be ending. Price fails to make a new extreme and instead breaks the prior counter-trend swing point. This is the "something changed" signal — in SMC/ICT it often triggers entries or exits.

How To Spot One

  1. Identify swing points — Scan left to right and mark obvious peaks (highs) and troughs (lows). Don't overthink it; roughly correct is fine.
  2. Connect the dots — In an uptrend, each subsequent peak should be higher (HH) and each subsequent trough should be higher (HL). Downtrend is the opposite.
  3. Find BOS — Price breaks above the most recent HH (in an uptrend). Trend continues.
  4. Find CHoCH — Price breaks below the most recent HL (in an uptrend) or above the most recent LH (in a downtrend). Trend may have changed.

Always check higher timeframes before acting on a BOS or CHoCH. A CHoCH on the 5-minute chart might be a minor blip on the 1-hour chart.

Common Pitfalls

  • Mistaking a pullback for CHoCH. Not every retracement is a reversal. A pullback that holds above the prior HL is healthy. CHoCH requires breaking it.
  • Ignoring timeframe context. The same price point can be a CHoCH on the 5-minute chart and noise on the 1-hour chart. Always read top-down.
  • Subjective structure. Different traders draw different swing points. Use algorithms or clear rules to reduce subjectivity — AlphaLoop's Structure Matrix does exactly this.
  • Chasing BOS. Jumping in at the first BOS often buys the top. BOS confirms the trend exists, it's not an entry signal. Wait for a retest.
