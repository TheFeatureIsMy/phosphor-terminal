Multi-Timeframe & Shadow Window

Concept · 07 / 10 · 8 min

A low-timeframe (LTF) break is provisional — only the high-timeframe (HTF) candle close confirms it. The wait between is the "shadow window."
"Higher timeframes set the bias; lower timeframes refine the entry. Decisions made against the higher-timeframe trend tend to fail."— TradingStrategyGuides[1]

Why It Matters

This is the most common mistake beginners make: a beautiful breakout appears on the 5-minute chart, they jump in, and price reverses, trapping them at the top. The problem isn't the breakout call — it's that they didn't wait for the higher timeframe to "stamp" it.

The HTF (Higher TimeFrame) sets the macro direction — it's the referee. The LTF (Lower TimeFrame) fine-tunes the entry — it's the athlete. The athlete can sprint, but the points don't count until the referee blows the whistle. The shadow window is the time between the sprint and the whistle.

How To Spot One

  1. Pick your HTF / LTF pair — Common combos: 1h + 5m, 4h + 15m, daily + 1h. HTF sets the bias; LTF refines the entry.
  2. HTF candle is still in progress — The current HTF candle has not yet closed. Any LTF structure break or signal you see is "provisional."
  3. Observe the shadow window — From the LTF signal until the HTF candle closes — that's the shadow window. Price may oscillate, wick around, or even reverse.
  4. Wait for HTF close confirmation — Once the HTF candle closes, if price is still favorably positioned (e.g., above a key structure level), the LTF signal is validated.

The core idea: patience beats speed. You don't need to catch the first breakout tick. Waiting for HTF confirmation may cost you a few pips, but it dramatically improves win rate.

Common Pitfalls

  • Trading inside the shadow window. Price action during the window is often noise. Making decisions here is like betting before the result is in. Wait for the close.
  • Giving up because price "got away." Even if price has moved after confirmation, it's still safer than entering blind inside the window. Don't abandon the plan because the price looks worse.
  • Wrong HTF choice. If your HTF is too large (e.g., daily), the shadow window can be hours long — unsuitable for intraday trading. Match the HTF to your holding period.
  • Ignoring HTF close quality. The close isn't just a timestamp — read the candle: wick length, body size, engulfing patterns. Same close price, different candle shape, different meaning.
