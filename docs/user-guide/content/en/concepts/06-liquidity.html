Liquidity, Sweeps, BSL / SSL

Concept · 06 / 10 · 6 min

Liquidity is where stop orders and resting limits pile up. Big players need that counterparty flow to fill — so they go hunt it.
"Buy-side liquidity sits above swing highs (where shorts stop out); sell-side liquidity sits below swing lows (where longs stop out)."— DailyPriceAction[1]

Why It Matters

Price doesn't move randomly — it gravitates toward the densest pools of liquidity. Stop hunts aren't the market "out to get you." Big players need counterparty flow to fill large orders. Understanding where liquidity sits explains why price visits certain levels and why it reverses there.

BSL (Buy-Side Liquidity) sits above swing highs — where short sellers' stop losses accumulate. SSL (Sell-Side Liquidity) sits below swing lows — where long holders' stops pile up. Before pushing price in their true direction, institutions often sweep these stops first to absorb the liquidity they need.

How To Spot One

  1. Mark clear swing points — Find the most recent Swing High and Swing Low on the chart.
  2. Draw BSL / SSL lines — BSL above the swing high; SSL below the swing low.
  3. Watch for "fishing" — Price briefly breaks past BSL/SSL then reverses immediately, leaving a long wick or engulfing candle. Classic stop-hunt.
  4. Wait for confirmation — If the break closes inside the range, liquidity was swept. If it closes outside, it may be a genuine breakout — re-evaluate.

Liquidity sweeps often cluster around major news events or session opens (Asia / London / NY) — that's when order flow is thickest.

Common Pitfalls

  • Every swing point is liquidity. Not every high/low has enough resting orders. Focus on obvious levels where price consolidated or tested multiple times.
  • Entering immediately after a sweep. Price can drift further before reversing. Wait for a secondary confirmation structure (e.g., CHoCH) for a safer entry.
  • Ignoring multi-timeframe liquidity. A BSL on the 5-minute chart might be noise on the 1-hour. Always check higher timeframes to find "the important liquidity pool."
  • Confusing sweeps with breakouts. A genuine breakout closes outside the level. A sweep closes inside, leaving a tail. Check the close.
