Order Blocks

Concept · 04 / 10 · 6 min

An Order Block marks where large players left unfilled orders — usually the last opposing candle before a strong move.
"An Order Block is a price zone where institutional traders have placed large pending orders. It is typically identified as the last opposing candle before a strong directional move — a zone where price is expected to return and react."— TradeThePool[1]

Why It Matters

Imagine a large institution wants to short 1,000 Bitcoin. It can't dump everything at market price like a retail trader — that would crater the price and fill at terrible levels. Instead, it pushes price higher, plants a wall of sell orders at a specific level, and waits for the market to come to it.

That "planted" level is the Order Block. It's typically the last candle opposite to the trend direction just before the trend starts. Find it, and you can place your orders where the institutions placed theirs — standing on the same side of the trade.

Order Blocks are the most common entry tool in SMC/ICT. Structure analysis (BOS/CHoCH) tells you the direction; Order Blocks tell you the price level.

How To Spot One

  1. Find a strong directional move — a big candle or a series of candles moving aggressively in one direction.
  2. Look for the last opposing candle — right before the move started, find the candle that went the other way. That's your potential Order Block.
  3. Mark the range — the OB zone is from the candle's high to its low.
  4. Wait for price to return — markets often retrace into the OB zone after a strong move, looking for liquidity.
  5. Watch the reaction — if price shows a reversal signal (wick, engulfing pattern) inside the OB zone, consider entering.

For a bearish OB, look for the last green candle before a downtrend. For a bullish OB, the last red candle before an uptrend.

Common Pitfalls

  • Every pullback candle is not an OB. It must be immediately adjacent to the start of a strong trend move — not just any counter-trend candle.
  • OB zone too wide. If the opposing candle has a huge range, the OB zone becomes too large to trade. Wait for price to test the 50% level or a narrower sub-zone.
  • Price may not return. Not every OB gets retested. Sometimes the trend runs away without you. That's fine — missing is better than forcing a bad entry.
  • Ignore the timeframe at your own risk. OB on a low timeframe is far less reliable than on 1H, 4H, or daily. Stick to higher timeframes.
